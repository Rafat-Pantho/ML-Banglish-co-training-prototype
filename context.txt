## Slide 2: Project Motivation & Research Question

### PROJECT MOTIVATION

#### The Banglish Challenge

Banglish (Bangla-English code-mixing) is the dominant form of online communication in Bangladesh. However, it presents unique ML hurdles:

* **Orthographic Chaos:** No standardized spelling (e.g., "khacchi", "khacci", "khachi").


* **Phonetic Consistency:** Despite spelling variations, the underlying pronunciation remains stable.


* **Deep Learning Overkill:** High compute requirements for Transformers are often unnecessary for simpler tasks like sentiment.


* **Image Caption:** Social Media Banglish Examples



### RESEARCH QUESTION

> "Can a phonetic-aware multi-view co-training approach match deep learning performance while remaining compute-efficient?"
> 
> 

* **Efficiency:** Leveraging shallow classifiers (SVM, Logistic Regression) for low-resource deployment.


* **Phonetic View:** Testing if phonetic encoding can serve as a truly independent view for co-training.

## Slide 3: Proposed Methodology & Strategy

### PROPOSED METHODOLOGY

*(Diagram visualizing feature matrices, Encoder E, Decoder D, Generator G, vector spaces $x_1, x_2, x_3$ and latent representations $h_1, h_2, h_3$)*

#### PMVC-WNM Strategy

The core innovation is representing Banglish text in two complementary, independent feature spaces:

* **View A: Character n-grams**
Captures orthography and common typos (3-gram/4-gram features).


* **View B: Phonetic Codes**
Captures pronunciation (Bangla Phonetic Encoding/Soundex).

## Slide 4: View B & The Co-training Loop

### VIEW B: PHONETIC ENCODING

#### Mapping Rules

Custom rules to collapse Bangla consonants into phonetic classes, neutralizing spelling errors.

| Class | Bangla Consonants | Code |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gutturals | কখগঘ | G |
| Palatals | চ, ছ জ, ঝ | J |
| Retroflex | ট, ঠ, ড, ঢ | T |
| Vowels | All Vowels | V |

* **Image Caption:** Soundex and BPE concept diagram



### THE CO-TRAINING LOOP

1. **Initialization:** Train $f_A$ and $f_B$ on small labeled set $L$ (800 samples).


2. **Pseudo-labeling:** Classifiers predict labels for unlabeled set $U$ (10,000 samples).


3. **Cross-Teaching:** High confidence examples from $f_A$ are added to training of $f_B$ and vice versa.


4. **Convergence:** Repeat for $ST=10S$ iterations until prediction stability.

## Slide 5: Weakly Supervised Noise Model & Dataset Setup

### WEAKLY SUPERVISED NOISE MODEL

#### Adaptive Noise Injection

Disagreement between $f_A$ and $f_B$ signals spelling distortions. We build a transition matrix to model these errors:

* **Image:** Noise transition matrix concept



$$PC_{obs}\vert{}P_{true}=\frac{count(C_{obs},P_{true})}{\Sigma\ count(C_{k},P_{true})}$$

Injecting this noise during training makes the character classifier robust to social media "typos".

### DATASET & SETUP

* **800:** Labeled Samples (Seed)


* **10K:** Unlabeled Comments


* **5K:** N-gram Features
